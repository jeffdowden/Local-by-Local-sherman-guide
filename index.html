1The honest overview
Small town charm.
Big growth aspirations.
Sherman is not trying to be Dallas. That's the whole point. It's a town where you still know your neighbors, where the guy at the hardware store remembers your name, and where Friday nights feel like something that actually belongs to the people who live here.
"The people moving here now are getting in while it still feels like home — before it becomes the next place everyone wishes they had bought sooner."
Jeff Dowden · 10 years in Sherman

Who thrives here
Families who want more house for their money without sacrificing quality of life. DFW transplants tired of the commute and the cost. Remote workers who realized they don't need to be 40 miles from downtown to live well. People who want to be part of something growing without being swallowed by it.
What Sherman is not
It's not a suburb. It's not finished — and that's a feature, not a bug. If you need a Whole Foods and rooftop bars, you'll be frustrated. If you want a real community where your dollar goes further and the pace is human — you'll wonder why you waited.
The commute reality
Sherman to Plano or Frisco is 45–55 minutes. To downtown Dallas, 65–75. For most people that's the trade — a commute two or three days a week in exchange for a home that's genuinely theirs, in a town with actual character.
The thing nobody puts in the brochure
Sherman is in the middle of one of the most significant economic transformations of any small Texas city in a generation. The window to get in before everyone else figures this out is closing — not tomorrow, but it's closing.

8Neighborhoods decoded
Where should
you actually live?
The honest answer depends almost entirely on your budget and life stage. Here's how I think about it after 138 transactions across Grayson County.
$200K–$300K
First-time buyers and growing families
Sherman's bread and butter. Established neighborhoods, mature trees, good school districts. Real yards. New construction is entering this range too. You get significantly more house than anywhere in DFW — the trade is you're in Sherman instead of Frisco. Most people who make that trade don't feel like it's a trade at all.
$300K–$450K
Move-up buyers and DFW transplants
Newer construction, larger lots, sometimes custom finishes at a price point that gets you a townhome in Frisco. This is the TI workforce relocation range. Also where you see the most appreciation as the market matures.
$450K+
Acreage, character, and the Sherman premium
You start getting into real land. Acreage properties, rural estate lots, homes built to last. Lake Texoma proximity. This range has seen the most appreciation over the last decade and has the most to gain from continued growth.

10The real numbers
What $300K actually
gets you here
The numbers that matter when you're making a real decision about where your family lives next.
$300K in Sherman vs DFW
In Sherman — 3 or 4 bedrooms, a real yard, two-car garage, established neighborhood. In Frisco — a townhome with an HOA that decides what color you paint your door. That math is the reason this market keeps growing.
Cost of living
Property taxes in Grayson County run roughly 2–2.5%. No state income tax. Grocery, dining, and services are noticeably lower than DFW. Most families report their monthly cost of living dropping meaningfully after the move.
The remote work equation
If you're fully remote or hybrid, Sherman's value proposition is almost unfair. Real community, outdoor access, lower cost of living, and a housing market where your dollar actually means something. No commute math required.
Schools — the honest take
Sherman ISD, Denison ISD, and Howe ISD are the primary options. Each has genuine strengths. This deserves a real conversation based on your specific situation — a general answer doesn't do it justice. Ask me directly.
